A blog is

> A frequently updated website, typically run by a single person and consisting of personal observations arranged in chronological order, excerpts from other sources, hyperlinks to other sites, etc.; an online journal or diary;
>
> -- [Oxford English Dictionary](https://www.oed.com/dictionary/blog_n)

So, in principle, I can write about anything on this blog. However, my original purpose for starting it was to take notes. While I use several note-taking apps for private notes, I also wanted a space to share some of my notes publicly. Writing in [Markdown](https://www.markdownguide.org/) is my preferred method, so using [Jekyll](https://jekyllrb.com/) to turn these files into blog posts felt like a natural and intentional choice.

Most of the notes I share here will be book notes since I read almost every day. My main note-taking method is *quotes plus commentary*—I like to clearly separate the book’s content from my own thoughts. That said, this isn’t a strict rule. Sometimes I’ll write summaries or let my thoughts wander into more reflective or imaginative territory.

[GPT-4o](https://openai.com/index/hello-gpt-4o/) evaluates my reading note-taking method like this:

> This approach strikes a thoughtful balance between structure and flexibility. By using *quotes plus commentary*, you create a clear dialogue between the author’s ideas and your own, which not only sharpens understanding but also encourages deeper reflection. At the same time, allowing space for summaries and spontaneous reflections keeps the process fluid and personal. It’s a method that treats reading as both an intellectual and creative act—engaging with the text while also allowing your own voice to emerge.
