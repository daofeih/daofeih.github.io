This blog serves multiple purposes, but the original purpose is for note-taking. It is a record of my intellectual journey, a way to engage with the books and ideas I encounter, and a tool for sharpening my thinking.

Reading is not just about absorbing information; it is an active process of engagement, reflection, and synthesis. So my note-taking method follows a simple yet effective structure: **reading-thinking-writing**. I read a section of a book or an article, pause to think deeply about its meaning and implications, and then write down my notes. This process forces me to process ideas critically rather than passively consume them.

I view the brain as a filter. Not everything we read should be absorbed uncritically. Some ideas should be examined, some refined, and others discarded. Writing helps clarify what is worth keeping. By putting my thoughts into words, I ensure that I truly understand what I read rather than just remembering isolated facts.
