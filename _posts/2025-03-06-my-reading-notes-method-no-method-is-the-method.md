I once tried to find the perfect reading notes strategy, hoping for a fixed format that would make my notes clear and systematic. But after repeated attempts, I realized that what truly matters is not the format but the act of recording itself.

So, I have abandoned the obsession with specific methods and opted for the simplest and most direct approach:

1. **Open the book and start reading**—without expectations or a rigid approach, just fully immersing myself in the text.
2. **Write down thoughts at any time**—anything that resonates with me or provokes deep thinking gets recorded immediately, without worrying about structure.
3. **Excerpt favorite passages or sentences**—whether long or short, if a passage moves me or holds significance, I jot it down.

This approach gives me greater freedom in reading, freeing me from the worry of “how to take notes.” I no longer stress about following the “excerpt + commentary” model, nor do I impose a fixed structure on my notes. Instead, I let my thoughts and feelings guide the recording process.

I will publish my reading notes on my blog, writing a post for each book I read. This is not only a way to organize my reading but also a means of refining my thoughts. My blog is more than just a place to store notes—it is a space to document the flow of my thinking.

Perhaps this is the reading notes method that suits me best: no method is the method.
