This is a personal blog, so I can post anything in principle, but the original purpose of this blog is to serve as a note-taking system for my reading. Here, I engage with books, ideas, and reflections in a way that deepens my understanding and allows for meaningful interaction with knowledge. My goal is not just to collect information but to internalize it, connect it with other ideas, and critically examine it. The best way I have found to achieve this is through an evolving method I call "reading-thinking-recording."

## The Reading-Thinking-Recording Method

This method is my way of making reading an active and engaging process. Instead of passively consuming text, I interact with it through structured thinking and writing. Here are the detailed steps:

### 1. Reading

- Read in large sections rather than stopping too frequently. This allows immersion in the author's ideas without disrupting the flow.
- Avoid mechanical note-taking while reading. Instead, focus on comprehension and engagement with the material.
- If something stands out, pause to let it settle in your mind before writing anything.

### 2. Thinking

- After reading a section, take a moment to reflect on what was just read.
- Ask yourself key questions:
  - What is the main idea or argument?
  - Do I agree or disagree, and why?
  - How does this connect with what I already know?
  - What new insights have I gained?
- If needed, revisit the text to clarify understanding before proceeding to the next step.

### 3. Recording

- Write down thoughts rather than mere summaries. The goal is not to simply restate but to engage with the content.
- Use a flexible structure:
  - **Excerpts** (if a passage is particularly striking or worth revisiting).
  - **Commentary** (personal thoughts, agreements, disagreements, connections to other ideas).
  - **Reflections** (broader insights, questions for future exploration, implications of the ideas).
- Don't force a rigid format; the key is to capture the essence of thinking in the moment.

## Why This Method Works for Me

This approach prevents my reading from becoming passive or mechanical. By ensuring that I think before writing, I avoid taking shallow notes. Instead, I create records of intellectual engagement—notes that I can return to not just as references but as traces of my evolving thought process.

Through this method, my blog becomes more than a collection of notes; it becomes a living record of learning and inquiry. I invite you to follow along, engage with these ideas, and perhaps develop your own approach to reading and thinking.
