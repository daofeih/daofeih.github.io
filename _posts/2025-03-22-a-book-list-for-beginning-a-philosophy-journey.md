I asked [GPT-4o](https://openai.com/index/hello-gpt-4o/) to introduce some books to me as a starting point for learning philosophy, and here is its answer:

> A great starting point for learning philosophy is:
>
> **"Sophie's World" by Jostein Gaarder**
>
> #### 🌍 What it is:
>
> A novel that doubles as a history of philosophy. It follows a teenage girl named Sophie who begins receiving mysterious letters that introduce her to philosophical ideas, from ancient Greece to modern times.
>
> #### 📚 Why it's a great starting point:
>
> - **Engaging format**: It reads like a mystery novel, making complex ideas approachable.
> - **Chronological journey**: You'll get a clear, structured overview of Western philosophy.
> - **Accessible language**: No prior knowledge required—perfect for beginners.
> - **Inspiration to go deeper**: You'll come away with a list of thinkers and ideas you’ll want to explore more seriously.
>
> #### 🧠 After reading it, you can explore:
>
> - **Bertrand Russell’s _The Problems of Philosophy_** – A short and clear classic.
> - **Edward Craig’s _Philosophy: A Very Short Introduction_** – A modern, thoughtful overview.
> - **Plato’s _The Republic_** – One of the foundational texts, more rewarding after an intro like this.
