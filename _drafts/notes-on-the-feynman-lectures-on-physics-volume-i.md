## Chapter 4. Conservation of Energy

### 4–2 Gravitational potential energy

> I wish to discuss the formula for gravitational energy near the surface of the Earth, and I wish to derive this formula in a way which has nothing to do with history but is simply a line of reasoning invented for this particular lecture to give you an illustration of the remarkable fact that a great deal about nature can be extracted from a few facts and close reasoning. It is an illustration of the kind of work theoretical physicists become involved in. It is patterned after a most excellent argument by Mr. Carnot on the efficiency of steam engines.

Starting from the most basic principles and obtaining some specific results through lengthy calculations, this kind of deductive reasoning is useful during exams. However, in the actual work of theoretical physicists, they are faced with unexplained phenomena, and they need to use inductive methods to "invent" some principles to explain the phenomena, and these principles could be allowed to be not so basic.

Feynman derived the form of gravitational potential energy through a thought experiment about weight-lifting machines. A weight-lifting machine is a machine that lifts one weight by lowering another.

Next, we need to define perpetual motion. For a perpetual motion machine, if it goes through a process and returns to its initial state, its overall effect is to do "work" on the outside. Specific to weight-lifting machines, its overall effect is to lift a weight. In this way, we can continue to operate the lifting machine with this lifted weight.

*Perpetual motion machines do not exist!* This is a principle induced by countless failed attempts to create a perpetual motion machine. We will use this principle to deduce the efficience of a reversible weight-lifting machine.

Suppose there are two weight-lifting machines A and B, and B is reversible. A lifts a weight of 3 units a distance X by lowering a unit weight a unit distance, and B lists the same weight a distance Y by lowering a unit weight a unit distance. Assuming $X > Y$, then we can lower the weight from X to Y, obtaining a "free power". Next, we use machine B to lower the weight a distance Y, so that it lifts the unit weight a unit distance. So the combined machine restores to the initial state, and the net result is to obtain a "free power". We can use the "free power" to run the machine again. This result is conflicted with the principle of the non-existence of perpetual motion machines. Thus, $X \leq Y$, i.e. reversible weight-lifting machines are the most effective. By letting A be a reversible machine, we can derive that $X = Y$, i.e. all reversible weight-lifting machines are the same effective. This is a universal law since it doesn't involve the inside mechanism of these machines.

Next, Feynman designed a reversible machine to calculate the X:

By applying the principle of the non-existence of perputual motion machines, we can derive that $3X \leq 1$. By running backwards the machine, we conclude that $1 \leq 3X$. Thus, $3X = 1$.

So the weight multiplying the height is conservative, and we call it as gravitational potential energy.
