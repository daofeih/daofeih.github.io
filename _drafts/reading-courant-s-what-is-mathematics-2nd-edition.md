[*What Is Mathematics?: An Elementary Approach to Ideas and Methods*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/What_Is_Mathematics%3F) (1941) By [Richard Courant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Courant) & [Herbert Robbins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herbert_Robbins) is a collection of gems of mathematics. The authors aimed to put meaning back to mathematics, i.e., to show that mathematics is not just a formal reasoning system.

[Ian Stewart](https://ianstewartjoat.weebly.com/) wrote a new chapter on the recent development of mathematics in the second edition (1996).

## PREFACE TO SECOND EDITION by Ian Stewart

Ian Stewart said that the authors of this book wanted to put meaning back to mathematics. It means that mathematics is not just a formal reasoning system in which concepts and postulates are created by free will. No! There are relations between mathematics and reality. Mathematics is a model of the real world.

A theorem is forever true once it is proven, but it doesn’t mean that mathematics is a dead subject. To show the rapid development of mathematics, Ian Stewart added a new chapter in the second edition. However, Ian Stewart didn’t include some interesting new topics such as chaos and broken symmetry. We can find these topics in his book [*From Here to Infinity*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/From_Here_to_Infinity_(book)).
