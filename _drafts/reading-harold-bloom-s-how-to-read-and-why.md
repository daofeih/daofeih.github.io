Harold Bloom's *How to Read and Why* was published in 2000.

"Reading", in the context of this book, means reading imaginative literature. The author thought that reading can help us defeat loneliness. Reading well means to make what is implicit in a book explicit. The author teaches us how and why to read by selecting some gems in literature, including poems, short stories, novels, and plays.

## Prologue: Why Read?

My answer: For pleasure, for otherness literature provides, and for improving myself.

But how to read? Here is the author's answer:

> find what comes near to you that can be put to the use of weighing and considering, and that addresses you as though you share the one nature, free of time's tyranny. Pragmatically that means, first find Shakespeare, and let him find you.

I know how to find Shakespeare, but how to understand "let him find you"? It might mean that I should read it with a fully open mind.
