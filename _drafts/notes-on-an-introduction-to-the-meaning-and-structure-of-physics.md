## On the Problem of Motion

### 1. The Tower of Pisa

Civilization is very fragile. After the decline of the once glorious Greek civilization, only a few of its original documents were left in Europe; there were a large number of translations of Greek documents in the Arab world, which were translated into Latin and then flowed back to Europe, which led to the Renaissance. So today's Europe understands Greek civilization through translations that have been passed on several times; to what extent can they get close to the original meaning of Greek documents? It is impossible to evaluate. This also made me start to reflect on one of my paranoias: my English is only half-baked, but I try to read the original English works rather than the translations. Maybe I can get more from reading the translations than from reading the original works. There is no shame in reading translations.
