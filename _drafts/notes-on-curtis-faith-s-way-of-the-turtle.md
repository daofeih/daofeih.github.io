> ..., *this is one of the five best trading books ever written*.

## Chapter 1. Risk Junkies

The difference between investors and traders:

> Investors are people who buy things for the long haul with the idea that over a considerable period—many years—their investments will appreciate in value.

> Traders care about price; essentially they buy and sell risk.

Financial markets have a function of transfering risk:

> In his informative and engaging book [*Against the Gods: The Remarkable Story of Risk*](https://book.douban.com/subject/2126074/), Peter Bernstein discusses how markets developed to allow the transfer of risk from one party to another. This is indeed the reason financial markets were created and a function they continue to serve.

### Traders Trade Risk

Two types of risk:

- Liquidity risk: the risk that a trader will not be able to buy or sell.
- Price risk: the possibility that prices will move significantly up or down.

In the market of an asset, there are two prices: the bid price and the ask price. Usually, the ask price would be higher than the bid price. The difference between them is called the spread. You have to buy the asset at the ask price and sell the asset at the bid price. The spread is the cost of buying or selling the asset, thus, the larger the spread, the smaller the liquidity. Traders trading liquidity risk are called scalpers or market makers.

Traders trading price risk are called speculators or position traders.
